Biomes

Overview

This is biomes in Subnautica. Dunes, Blood Kelp Islands, Mountains, Bulb Zone, Crash Site, and Grand Reef should all be approached with caution. The Dead Zone lies beyond these biomes, an empty biome with only three Void Ghost Leviathans ready to attack. This biome should be avoided at all costs; it has nothing of interest.


Early development footages of biome generation can be seen on a playlist from Russel Maekim's YouTube channel. The game considers the inside of an Observatory, Lifepod, Scanner Room, Alien Containment, and inside the aquarium in the Primary Containment Facility to be completely separate biomes. Early surface biome concepts were divided into Kelp Forest, Dense Kelp Forest, Sunny Kelp Forest, Coral Reef, Danger Reef, Safe Shallows, Grand Reefs, and Floating Islands. Early lava zone biome concepts were divided into Lava Zone, Lava Bridges, Inactive Lava Zones, and Thermal Vents. The Lava Lakes and the Crater Edge are the only biomes that do not contain any Flora.


There are many Biomes to explore and exploit in Subnautica. They're all miniature replicas of real-world ecosystems. The brilliant Kelp Forest to the boiling depths of the intriguing Lava Lakes are just a few of the Biomes available. Each Biome has its own Flora and Fauna to find, as well as resources to harvest.
