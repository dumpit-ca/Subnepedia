Oxygen

The player requires oxygen (O2) in order to survive. When diving, the player consumes an oxygen supply that depletes over time. If the player runs out of oxygen, their screen will begin to fade to black, giving them around 4 seconds to find an air source before the screen fades fully out and they die.

Sources of Oxygen

  • Brain Coral

    • In Subnautica, brain corals produce bubbles that float to the surface and provide the player oxygen if they touch them.

  • Ice Bubbles

    • Ice bubbles are a source of oxygen found only in the Arctic and underwater areas of the Glacial Basin in Subnautica: Below Zero. They require the user to use a Laser Cutter to open them, and once opened, they will release a steady stream of oxygen bubbles.

  • Oxygen Plant

    • Oxygen Plants are a flora species found in several deep biomes of Sector Zero in Subnautica: Below Zero. The player gets oxygen by interacting with the plant's bulb.

  • Titan Holefish

    • Titan Holefish are a fauna species present in Subnautica: Below Zero's Sparse Arctic and two Arctic Regions. They are slow and passive swimmers, but they are surrounded by dangerous Symbiotes who will attack the player if they are approached. The player gets oxygen by interacting with the oxygen-filled ring inside their bodies.

PDA

Personal Digital Assistant

The PDA (Personal Digital Assistant), often known as the Survival PDA, is a multifunctional device carried by the player at all times. The PDA's AI monitors the player's health and will notify them whether they have 30 or 10 O2 points left, or if they are on the verge of dehydration or famine. When the player enters new places or finds new items, it also provides vital information.

  1. Inventory paper doll

    2. The player can equip goods using a paper doll interface on the PDA's inventory tab. The head, torso, feet, and hands all have equipment slots, as well as an air tank and two computer chips. In order for equipment to help the player, it must be placed in the appropriate slot.

  2. Blueprints

    3. The blueprints tab displays the player's current blueprints as well as their associated recipes.

  3. Log messages

    4. While exploring Subnautica and Below Zero, you can come across abandoned PDAs. Collecting abandoned PDAs causes the player's PDA to download the log messages. Other characters will occasionally phone the player on the PDA in Below Zero.

  4. Databank

    5. The player's databank records are saved to the PDA's Databank tab. The AI may periodically notify the player that a new databank entry is available and will describe the information on rare occasions.
