Getting Started

Overview

Welcome to the Subnautica Getting Started Guide for newbie players who are experiencing difficulty or are just ready to survive. The Exploration Tips page may also be useful.


In Subnautica you play as a high-tech castaway. You begin the game fleeing in Lifepod 5.


If you opt to play in "Survival mode," you must eat and drink on a regular basis, but you have an infinite number of lives. If you die, your inventory will be emptied immediately, and you will respawn in the last habitat you entered before death, with the same inventory you had at the time. You start out without any tools, but you do have your PDA, which the game tells you to use.


All hostile life-forms are capable of killing you, although practically none do it instantaneously. This allows you to flee before they can finish you off.

Venturing The Ocean

When you are going out for an expedition, always bring only food, water, batteries, and tool you need. Taking few items will help you to have larger space in your inventory while in expedition.

Sit Down

Food and water don't tick down if you're sitting in a chair. At first, you will think thechair is for decorations but siiting on one helps to keep your stats down. Have a seat when cathching through your PDA.

Tiny Spaces

Plants pots are better than Growbeds, plant pots consume smaller place than growbeds. In this game space is one of the important thing to consider.

Pin It!

Always carry atleast two beacons, the game does not have an in-game map, also the underwater biomes do not exactly have landmarks or signposts.

Travel Light

You swim more faster when you carry less in your inventory. You can swim faster when you use the glider but its important to save its battery for emergency purposes.

Basic Necessities

Never forget the one basic thing about this game. Nothing stacks in this game so everything you pick up takes up its own separate inventory slot.

Organize Freak

When you start building your base, build a bunch of lockers so you have plenty of storage for your materials.
